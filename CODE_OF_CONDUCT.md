# Code of Conduct

This project adopts the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/).

## Our standards

**Expected behavior:**
- Be kind, be honest, be patient
- Critique code and motion design, never people
- Welcome contributors at every experience level
- Accept constructive feedback gracefully
- Credit others for their contributions and ideas

**Unacceptable behavior:**
- Harassment, discrimination, trolling, or personal attacks in any form
- Doxxing or sharing private information without consent
- Sustained disruption of issues, PRs, discussions, or community spaces
- Dismissing contributions without explanation
- Demanding immediate responses or escalating conflict when review takes time

## Project-specific norms

**In animation PRs:** Feedback on motion quality (SPEC compliance, easing character, choreography) is substantive technical feedback, not personal criticism. "This doesn't meet SPEC §3 quality bar" is always appropriate; "your taste is bad" is not.

**In bug reports:** Assume good faith. A user who filed a confusing issue is someone who spent time trying to help. Ask clarifying questions before closing. Answer with a minimal reproduction rather than a documentation link and nothing else.

**In RFC discussions:** Disagreement on technical direction is welcome and expected. Challenge ideas directly; do not impugn the proposer's competence or motives. "I think approach B is better because X" is productive. "Only someone who doesn't understand CSS would suggest this" is not.

**Response time:** This is maintained by a small team. Delays in review are not signals of rejection. If a PR or issue has had no response in 7 days, a single bump comment is appropriate.

## Enforcement

Report conduct issues to **hello@salkomdesignstudio.com** with subject `COC: sds-motion-forge`.

Reports are reviewed by the maintainers, kept confidential, and acknowledged within 7 days. Consequences scale from a private written warning to a temporary ban to a permanent ban, at the maintainers' judgment, following the [Contributor Covenant enforcement guidelines](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/#enforcement-guidelines).

If you believe a maintainer is the source of a conduct issue and cannot report to that maintainer directly, email the same address with `ESCALATION` in the subject line and we will identify an independent third party to review.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces: GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any community space referencing this project.
